Acrylic on Canvas

Concept/inspiration

Translation from photography to painting. First exploration of realism in acrylics, focusing on light and shadow.

Materials and techniques

Acrylic on canvas 30x40cm 2025

Challenges/discoveries

The initial idea was to divide a single canvas into alternating monochromatic sections (yellow and blue) to create a contrast within one piece.

After completing the yellow sections, I decided to separate the idea into two paintings instead, so I could play with Kandinsky’s theory of colour (how colour links to emotions and expressions).

Yellow: Strong, confident, energetic

Blue: Soft, vulnerable, calm

Challenges/discoveries

Figuring out how to thin acrylic paint effectively. Using too much water made the paint too runny, and different mediums affected the finish. Pouring medium thinned the paint but also changed matte paint to shiny. I learned how to balance the amount and type of thinning medium to achieve the desired fluidity and texture, maintaining control over the paint’s flow while keeping the intended look.

Watercolour

Concept/inspiration

Experimenting with new neon watercolours

Materials and techniques

Watercolour A4 2021

Challenges/discoveries

This was my first time properly experimenting with watercolour beyond basic shapes. I struggled with sketching because pencil lines remained visible underneath. To solve this, I created a sketch in black marker on a separate paper, placed behind my painting on a window as a guide. The limitation was that I couldn’t use thick watercolour paper for this method, so I worked on thinner paper, which wrinkled with the wetness.

Concept/inspiration

School project: silhouettes

Materials and techniques

Watercolour A4 2021

Challenges/discoveries

Creating a cloudlike effect in the sky took multiple attempts. I experimented with different techniques until I achieved the soft, blended look I wanted. I also explored how to create texture in watercolour and discovered that using salty water produced a unique waterdrop effect.
