Bookbinding

Concept/inspiration

Learning how to bookbind and trying different techniques

Materials and techniques

Bookbinding 2020

Challenges/discoveries

The first book I made was the brown one. I stained the paper and the cover fabric with coffee and black tea. I didn’t dilute the mixture enough, so the outcome was much darker than intended and still smells like coffee many years later. The first book also didn’t yet have a curved spine or head- and tailbands, so the edges look a bit rough.

In later books, I learned how to create a curved spine, add a ribbon marker, and sew in head- and tailbands. I also tried my hand at creating slightly three-dimensional appliqués on the covers. Through making books of varying sizes, I discovered that the direction of the paper matters a lot for the finished outcome: if the pages are sewn together in the wrong direction, they stand upright instead of falling to each side when the book is opened.
